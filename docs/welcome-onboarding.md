## The Project in Simple Terms

Think of this like **teaching a self-driving car by showing it how to drive**:

1. **Students drive** in a simulator (like a video game)
2. **The system watches** and learns from every drive
3. **The car gets better** at driving itself over time
4. **Everyone contributes** to making one shared model smarter

You don't need to be an expert! We need people at all skill levels to:

- Drive in the simulator and collect data
- Organize and clean the data
- Test the autonomous driving
- Build dashboards and visualizations
- Help with documentation and organization

## Key Concepts to Know

### Learning From Demonstration (LfD)

- **What it is**: Humans show the computer how to do something
- **In our project**: Students drive → computer learns → car drives itself
- **Why it matters**: This is how real-world ML systems often work

### The Improvement Cycle

1. **Collect data** → Students drive in simulator
2. **Train model** → System learns from all drives
3. **Test autonomously** → Watch the car drive itself
4. **Observe results** → See what works and what doesn't
5. **Add more data** → Drive more to fix problems
6. **Repeat** → Model gets better over time

### Team Collaboration

- **We're all building ONE model together**
- **Every drive helps the entire team**
- **Progress is visible and cumulative**
- **Different skills needed** (not just coding!)
